03 — Heading Hierarchy (Bad)

Levels are skipped and visual styling is used instead of semantics. Screen reader users get a broken table of contents.

← Back to overview
How to spot this: Firefox Accessibility Panel → "Check for issues" → look for heading hierarchy warnings. Screen reader: use H to jump between headings (NVDA) or the Rotor (VoiceOver) and notice the unexpected jumps.
Bad — broken heading hierarchy

Company Blog h1 — correct

Featured article h3 — h2 skipped!

This article looks like a section heading visually, but is marked up as h3. The h2 level is completely missing.

Author information h5 — h4 skipped!

Written by Jane Doe · 12 January 2025

More articles h2 — appears after h3 and h5!

A list of further reading. The heading level jumped backwards — a screen reader user navigating by heading has no way to understand the page structure.

Newsletter signup div — not a heading at all

Visually this looks like a heading. In the accessibility tree it is just a paragraph of text with no structural role.

What a screen reader user hears

When navigating by heading, the outline of this page looks like this:

h1  Company Blog
  h3  Featured article          ← where is h2?
    h5  Author information      ← where is h4?
  h2  More articles             ← back to h2 after h5?
  (no heading)  Newsletter      ← completely invisible

Compare this to the good example in 04 — Heading Hierarchy (Good).
